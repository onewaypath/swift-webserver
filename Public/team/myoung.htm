p>As Co-founder, Vice-President and Chief Design Ofﬁcer at One Way Path Group, Miriam oversees all design elements for projects sponsored by the company with an emphasis on promoting food security, active living, social cohesion and peacebuilding. An insight meditation teacher and registered nurse for over 12 years, Miriam applies her years of experience in the health care industry to community design.

Miriam’s work and dedication to peace building though insight meditation has been recognized by Thai governments both at the national and provincial levels. She is a recipient of the benefactor of Buddhism award from the governor of Chiangmai Province in 2013 and from the Princessof Thailand in 2018. She is co-founder and pro-bono teacher-inresidence at the Buddhavipassana Meditation Centre in Toronto.

Miriam earned an insight meditation teaching certificate from Chomtong Temple and Meditation Centre in Northern Thailand, training under the late Ven. Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo, Thanat Chindaporn and Kathryn Johnston Chindaporn. She holds an honours Bachelors of Science in Nursing from the University of Toronto and a Bachelor of Kinesiology form McMaster University. She is a member in good standing of the College of Nurses of Ontario (CNO#: 08358591).
